About Human Factors

Human Factors is a subdiscipline of psychology that deals with optimizing the technical systems to make them user-friendly. Over the course of my career, I have gained experiences in different qualitative methods of data collection like card sorting, the conduction and interpretation of interviews, and the analysis of online reviews. But quantitative research methods were also a huge part of my educational program. For instance, skin conductance level, heart rate, and eye-tracking.

Research Projects

Driving simulator study of the effects of a digital in-car tutor in automated cars

For my most notable research project, I investigated the effect of a visual and auditory tutor in highly automated cars on driving safety. For this purpose, I conducted a driving simulator study and analyzed reaction time, lane deviation, and glances away from the traffic situation.

Content analysis of physician online reviews

To investigate potential discrimination based on health insurance type, a content-analysis of 415 online reviews was conducted. Patients with governmental insurance were compared to and privately insured patients regarding their remarks of medical communication.

An eye-tracking study of menu-alignment

Most websites nowadays apply a left-aligned main menu, even though there is little evidence that suggests that this form of a menu is advantageous to other forms. Therefore, my research team conducted an eye-tracking study to investigate whether left-aligned menus are actually more user-friendly than right-aligned menus.

Design Projects

Front-end user interface design in in-car screens for a popular German car brand

During my role as UX specialist, I was responsible for designing and maintaining various functions in the digital user interface of one of Germany's top-selling premium car brands. However, for legal reasons, I cannot to address this part of my work in further detail.

Redesigning an Analysis Tool to estimate the values of vehicles

I was striving for a balance between modern design with usability. The number of input fields was reduced, and the drop-down selection menus were set by default to the most used settings. Besides that, additional visual cues were introduced, and the font was changed to be in line with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.

Redesigning a Complex Web Layout

The web service provides many features that I strived to integrate into one design in the most efficient way possible. The color design was changed, and widgets were modified and fixed on the home page. The input forms were made more user-friendly based on user data.

Designing a Signup Page

When I was assigned the task of redesigning the registration page, it was important to me that the new design is modern and at the same time looks very professional and trustworthy. I distributed input fields on a three-step registration process, only asking the user for a few inputs at a time to increase the perceived ease of use.
